Hybrid Endurance System

16-Week Half Marathon + Strength

Three primary runs, two required lifts, and one optional athletic lift. Running performance leads; strength preserves force, durability, and athleticism.

A progressive half-marathon plan for an experienced strength athlete. The plan builds threshold capacity, weekly mileage, and long-run durability while maintaining strength, power, unilateral control, rotation, calf capacity, and the movement variability needed for long-term athleticism.

Starting-point rule: This plan assumes a current running base of roughly 10–15 miles per week and a comfortable 4–5 mile long run. Reduce all run durations and distances by 15–25% during Weeks 1–4 if your current base is lower. Easy and long runs stay conversational. Tempo effort is controlled and repeatable, not a time trial.
